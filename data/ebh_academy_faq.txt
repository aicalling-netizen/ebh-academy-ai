Part 1 — Foundation &amp; Operating Principles
This document is the standard FAQ and counselling reference for EBH Academy. It is grounded in the verified Course Certification Matrix and the official CIDESCO syllabus. Use the same answer in chatbot replies, WhatsApp, and live counselling conversations — the AI calling-agent prompt handles tone and pacing separately.
Honesty Principles
We never promise a job, placement, salary, visa, residency or other outcome we do not control.
We never claim accreditation we do not hold. Every accreditation claim must align to the Course Certification Matrix in Part 3.
On DHA we always say 'prepares you to apply for the DHA licensing examination' — never 'gives you a DHA license' or 'automatic DHA licence.' Only the Diploma in Beauty Therapy is DHA-eligible.
We never quote 'average' salary or fee figures in chat. Verified current numbers are shared in the counselling session, in writing.
We never use more than ONE FOMO statement per conversation, and only after firm interest is shown.
If a student discloses distress, financial hardship, mental-health concerns or a difficult life situation, we pause conversion entirely and prioritise wellbeing. Care comes before any sale.
 
DHA Language Lock
ALWAYS: 'prepares you to apply for the DHA licensing examination' NEVER: 'you will be DHA-licensed' · 'gives you a DHA license' · 'automatic DHA' · 'DHA-certified' Per our verified Certification Matrix, only the Diploma in Beauty Therapy is DHA-eligible. DHA itself awards the license based on their own examination, eligibility and decision.
 
Information-First Conversation Style
Provide useful answers first; ask for contact details only when the student is ready. Part 2 — Our Faculty &amp; Multi-Disciplinary Expertise
EBH Academy is taught by a multi-disciplinary team intentionally built for the way the modern beauty, wellness and aesthetic industry actually works. You won't be taught by a single style of trainer; you'll learn from clinical, therapeutic, nutritional and commercial perspectives, each brought into the classroom where they genuinely add depth. This is one of the structural reasons our graduates feel ready to walk into salons, wellness spas and clinical environments with confidence.
 
Dubai Health Authority Approved Staff
Our teaching team includes DHA-approved professionals — recognised by the Dubai Health Authority as qualified to operate within their respective scopes. This is part of what allows EBH to deliver the Diploma in Beauty Therapy as a genuinely DHA-aligned educational pathway, and it gives students direct exposure to professionals who understand the realities of practising under DHA regulation in Dubai.
CIDESCO-Certified Beauty Therapy Trainers
Our practical Beauty Therapy education is led by CIDESCO-certified trainers who themselves hold the international Swiss-headquartered qualification they are teaching students to earn. They know the CIDESCO syllabus from inside — what the examiners look for, where students typically struggle, how to teach to mastery rather than to memorisation. Their direct CIDESCO credentialling is the assurance that what you learn in the classroom maps exactly to what CIDESCO will assess.
Medical Doctors
Where clinical depth genuinely matters — anatomy, physiology, contraindications, skin pathology, the science behind aesthetic treatments — our students learn from medical doctors. This brings the clinical perspective into the room and ensures that the science underpinning your practice is taught the way it would be taught in a clinical setting, not paraphrased from a textbook. This is one of the most quietly powerful differentiators of an EBH education.
Physiotherapist
Our team includes a physiotherapist — bringing structured knowledge of musculoskeletal anatomy, body mechanics, posture, soft-tissue work and the principles that make body therapy safe and effective over a long career. For massage and body therapy students this is invaluable: it's the difference between learning technique that looks right and learning technique that protects both the client's body and your own across a thirty-year career.
Dietitian
Wellness is no longer just about treatment — clients increasingly expect their therapist to understand the broader picture of health, nutrition and lifestyle. Our team includes a dietitian who brings the nutritional context that elevates a beauty therapist into a wellness advisor. This depth distinguishes you in consultation and creates genuine value for clients on holistic programmes.
MBA &amp; Marketing Expert
Especially for our Beauty &amp; Spa Management diploma — but also for any student thinking about freelance, home-service or entrepreneurship — our team includes an MBA-qualified marketing expert. Strategy, brand-building, customer acquisition, digital marketing, business planning and the commercial realities of running a profitable spa or salon are taught by someone who has actually done this work, not theorised about it. This is the layer that turns a technical therapist into a commercially-aware professional.
 
Why this faculty design matters
Every working day in modern Dubai brings a beauty professional into contact with clients who have medical histories, dietary concerns, structural posture issues, business questions and high expectations of professional consultation. A team built only of beauty trainers produces beauty technicians. A team built of beauty trainers, doctors, a physiotherapist, a dietitian and an MBA/marketing expert produces a different kind of graduate — one who can speak credibly across every dimension of the modern wellness conversation. That is the EBH faculty design, and that is what you will experience inside our classrooms.

Part 4 — CIDESCO Diploma in Beauty Therapy
The CIDESCO Diploma in Beauty Therapy is the most comprehensive qualification in the EBH catalogue and the only one that is DHA-eligible — meaning a graduate may pursue the DHA licensing examination separately to qualify for clinical settings in Dubai. The year is built around CIDESCO's globally respected curriculum and prepares you to walk into salons, wellness spas and clinics with the confidence of someone who has trained to a Swiss-headquartered international standard founded in 1946.
 
Accreditations
CIDESCO Switzerland · KHDA Dubai · IAO International · EBH Academy · DHA license pathway eligible
CIDESCO training hours
Minimum 1,200 hours of practical and theoretical training in a registered CIDESCO School (per CIDESCO official syllabus)
Examination format
CIDESCO examination has four components: Facial examination · Body examination · Project with Case Histories (2,500–4,000 words; pass mark 70%) · Written multiple-choice examination
Career destinations
Beauty therapist (salons, wellness spas, hotel &amp; resort spas); aesthetic-clinic-side roles via the DHA route; specialisation into clinical aesthetics; senior or lead therapist progression; in-house trainer over time; freelance, home-service and entrepreneurship
 
Full Module Breakdown
Each module below shows what is taught and why it matters — drawn from the official CIDESCO syllabus and the EBH delivery framework.
 
1. Natural Science Foundation
WHAT YOU LEARN: The scientific bedrock that everything else rests on — chemistry of cosmetic products, biology of cells and tissues, physics of skincare devices, and the principles behind hygiene and sterilisation in a clinical environment.
WHY IT MATTERS: Without this foundation, technique is just imitation. With it, you understand WHY a particular product or treatment works on a particular skin, and you can adapt safely in real client situations.
 
2. Anatomy &amp; Physiology
WHAT YOU LEARN: The body's major systems taught from a beauty-therapy perspective — skeletal, muscular, circulatory, lymphatic, nervous, integumentary, endocrine, digestive, respiratory and reproductive systems. Special focus on the systems most relevant to facial and body treatment.
WHY IT MATTERS: Real clients arrive with high blood pressure, hormonal conditions, recent surgeries, autoimmune conditions, pregnancy, medication interactions. Understanding the systems is what makes you a SAFE practitioner who knows when a treatment is appropriate, when to modify, and when to refer out. It's also a core component of the CIDESCO written examination.
 
3. Skin &amp; Dermatology
WHAT YOU LEARN: Skin structure, skin types, skin conditions, dermatological disorders, skin disorders versus skin diseases, contraindications, and the science of how skin behaves at every stage of life. Skin analysis methodology and Fitzpatrick classification.
WHY IT MATTERS: Reading skin properly is the single most valuable consultation skill in modern beauty therapy. Clients now expect sophisticated analysis before any treatment — therapists who can read skin properly build loyal, high-value clienteles. Therapists who can't will harm clients eventually.
 
4. Cosmetic Science
WHAT YOU LEARN: The chemistry of cosmetic and professional skincare products — active ingredients, formulations, pH and skin barrier function, ingredient compatibility, and product safety. Reading a label like a professional.
WHY IT MATTERS: You'll spend your career recommending products. Understanding what's actually in them — and what each ingredient does — separates a credible professional from a salesperson reading a brochure. It's also one of the most directly examined CIDESCO theory subjects.
 
5. Facial Treatments (Practical)
WHAT YOU LEARN: The full CIDESCO facial sequence: client consultation, deep cleansing, exfoliation, skin analysis, facial massage techniques (effleurage, petrissage, tapotement, friction), mask application and removal, serum and moisturiser application, complete facial protocol.
WHY IT MATTERS: Facials are the gateway treatment for almost every salon and spa career. CIDESCO's facial examination tests this skill formally — and your competence here will define your first impression on every clinical interviewer.
 
6. Electrical Facial Treatments
WHAT YOU LEARN: Professional electrical modalities used in facial treatment — galvanic current (desincrustation and iontophoresis), high-frequency, vacuum suction, microcurrent, and brush cleansing. Safe operation, contraindications, treatment design and equipment maintenance.
WHY IT MATTERS: Modern salons and aesthetic clinics increasingly expect electrical facial competence as standard. CIDESCO specifically tests electrical equipment in the facial examination, and the ability to deliver advanced facials commands premium pricing.
 
7. Body Treatments (Practical)
WHAT YOU LEARN: Body analysis, body massage techniques across the full body, body wraps and exfoliation, and the protocol for a professional body treatment from consultation to aftercare.
WHY IT MATTERS: Body work is the other major revenue stream in wellness spas. The body examination is one of the four CIDESCO assessment components, and competence here is what allows you to work in spa settings as well as facial-focused salons.
 
8. Electrical Body Treatments
WHAT YOU LEARN: Body-specific electrical modalities for contouring, lymphatic drainage support and muscle stimulation. Safety, contraindications, body analysis and treatment design.
WHY IT MATTERS: Body contouring and slimming services are some of the highest-margin treatments in the UAE wellness market. CIDESCO examines this formally as part of the body examination.
 
9. Hair Removal — Waxing
WHAT YOU LEARN: Strip waxing, hot waxing, hair growth cycles, contraindications, client comfort management, hygiene protocol, and waxing of all relevant body areas to professional standard.
WHY IT MATTERS: Waxing is part of the CIDESCO body examination and a high-volume, daily-bread service in virtually every salon. Competent, comfortable, hygienic waxing technique builds the regular client base every therapist needs early in their career.
 
10. Make-up Application
WHAT YOU LEARN: Skin preparation, foundation matching, contouring, eye make-up techniques, lip work, day and evening looks, and applying make-up suitable for different skin tones, ages and occasions.
WHY IT MATTERS: Make-up is part of the CIDESCO facial examination and one of the most client-facing services in beauty therapy — bridal, event and special-occasion make-up adds a meaningful revenue stream.
 
11. Eyelash &amp; Eyebrow Treatments
WHAT YOU LEARN: Eyelash and eyebrow tinting, eyebrow shaping (with tweezers and threading), brow design for face shapes, and the relevant patch-testing and safety protocols.
WHY IT MATTERS: Examined directly in the CIDESCO facial examination. Also one of the most-requested quick services in modern salons, with strong rebook rates.
 
12. Manicure &amp; Pedicure
WHAT YOU LEARN: Nail anatomy, nail conditions, manicure and pedicure technique to professional standard, nail polish application, cuticle and nail-bed care, and salon hygiene for nail services.
WHY IT MATTERS: Examined in the CIDESCO facial examination component. Many therapists offer this as a complementary service, particularly in salon settings.
 
13. Aesthetic Treatments — Theory
WHAT YOU LEARN: The theory of contemporary aesthetic treatments and where beauty therapy intersects with medical aesthetics, including the proper scope of practice for a beauty therapist (versus medical scope which requires DHA-licensed medical professionals).
WHY IT MATTERS: Knowing your scope of practice is what protects clients, your career and the wider profession. Examined in the CIDESCO written paper.
 
14. Hygiene, Sterilisation &amp; Safety
WHAT YOU LEARN: Cross-infection control, sterilisation methods (autoclave, chemical, disposable), salon hygiene protocols, blood-borne pathogen awareness, and client safety throughout treatment.
WHY IT MATTERS: CIDESCO practical examiners place heavy emphasis on hygiene during every practical exam. More importantly: poor hygiene ends careers and harms clients. Excellent hygiene is a hallmark of every respected professional.
 
15. Client Care, Consultation &amp; Communication
WHAT YOU LEARN: Professional client consultation, recording client history accurately, contraindication identification, treatment recommendation, professional language and presence, building rapport, and after-treatment care guidance.
WHY IT MATTERS: Specifically emphasised in CIDESCO practical examinations — examiners assess your client care and interaction throughout. In real practice, clients rebook the therapists who made them feel listened to, not just technically treated.
 
16. Business Studies
WHAT YOU LEARN: Foundations of running a beauty therapy practice — basics of pricing, client retention, professional standards, salon operations, and the commercial realities of a working career.
WHY IT MATTERS: Examined in the CIDESCO written paper. Also: every therapist eventually faces commercial decisions, whether negotiating a salary, freelancing or opening their own business.
 
17. Project with Case Histories
WHAT YOU LEARN: An independently researched written project of 2,500–4,000 words on a subject relevant to beauty therapy, connected to documented case histories of clients you have treated. Pass mark 70%.
WHY IT MATTERS: This component is what makes the CIDESCO Diploma genuinely a professional qualification — you are required to demonstrate research, clinical reasoning and case-tracking discipline, which is what separates a credentialled therapist from a technique-only graduate.
 
In Summary
By the time you graduate, you will not just hold a certificate — you will have practised on real models repeatedly, understood the science under your hands, and earned a credential that travels with you internationally. Many of our graduates tell us the year was harder than they expected and more rewarding than they had let themselves hope for. That's the journey we build for you.

Part 5 — CIDESCO Diploma in Body Therapy
The CIDESCO Diploma in Body Therapy is a focused, body-specialised pathway from CIDESCO Switzerland. Aimed at producing therapists who can work effectively in body therapy settings including body treatments with electrical treatments and massage. Shorter and more focused than the full Beauty Therapy diploma, it carries CIDESCO + IAO + EBH credentials. It is not a DHA clinical-licensing pathway — for that, the Diploma in Beauty Therapy is the route.
 
Accreditations
CIDESCO Switzerland · IAO International · EBH Academy
CIDESCO training hours
Minimum 565 hours at a registered CIDESCO School covering all subjects in the CIDESCO Body Therapy Syllabus (per CIDESCO official)
Examination format
CIDESCO examination has three components: Theoretical examination (Multiple Choice Questions) · Practical examination · Project with Case Histories. Candidates must also complete at least 4 school examinations (2 practical, 2 theory) during training before sitting the CIDESCO examination.
Career destinations
Body therapist in wellness spas, hotel and resort spas; specialisation in body contouring, lymphatic and wellness work; freelance and home-service body practice; complementary qualification for therapists wanting deeper body expertise
 
Full Module Breakdown
Each module below shows what is taught and why it matters — drawn from the official CIDESCO syllabus and the EBH delivery framework.
 
1. Anatomy &amp; Physiology — Body Focus
WHAT YOU LEARN: Deep coverage of the body systems most relevant to body therapy: skeletal, muscular, circulatory, lymphatic, nervous, integumentary and endocrine systems with particular focus on how body treatments interact with these systems.
WHY IT MATTERS: Body work demands a confident understanding of what's happening beneath your hands — muscle groups, lymphatic drainage routes, contraindications. This module is what makes you a safe and effective body therapist.
 
2. Body Analysis &amp; Consultation
WHAT YOU LEARN: Structured body assessment, postural analysis, identifying body conditions and goals, recording client history, contraindication identification, treatment planning and goal-setting with the client.
WHY IT MATTERS: Body work is increasingly consultation-led and clients want a real plan, not a generic treatment. Strong consultation skill is what builds repeat bookings and treatment loyalty.
 
3. Body Massage Techniques
WHAT YOU LEARN: Foundation and intermediate body massage technique — effleurage, petrissage, friction, tapotement, vibration — flow design, pressure modulation, treatment sequencing, draping protocol and treatment-room hygiene.
WHY IT MATTERS: Body massage is the core practical skill the CIDESCO body therapy examination tests. Technique mastery here is what makes you employable across wellness settings.
 
4. Body Treatments — Wraps, Scrubs &amp; Exfoliation
WHAT YOU LEARN: Body wraps, salt and sugar scrubs, exfoliation protocols, body masks and the professional sequencing of complete body treatments from consultation to aftercare.
WHY IT MATTERS: Modern wellness spas build their menus around body treatments. Competence here makes you immediately useful in a spa environment.
 
5. Electrical Body Treatments
WHAT YOU LEARN: Body-specific electrical modalities for contouring, lymphatic support, muscle stimulation and skin tone. Safe operation, treatment design, contraindications and equipment care.
WHY IT MATTERS: Electrical body treatments deliver visible results clients pay premium for. CIDESCO examines this formally and salons increasingly require this competence.
 
6. Spa Wellness Concepts
WHAT YOU LEARN: Understanding spa and wellness culture, holistic treatment philosophy, client wellness journey design, integration of body work with broader wellness practices.
WHY IT MATTERS: Wellness has become the dominant frame for body work in modern spas. Therapists who understand the wellness context get more interesting roles than those who only know the techniques.
 
7. Hygiene, Sterilisation &amp; Safety
WHAT YOU LEARN: Cross-infection control, sterilisation methods, professional hygiene protocols and client safety throughout body treatment work.
WHY IT MATTERS: CIDESCO practical examiners assess hygiene throughout. In real practice, hygiene reputation is your career reputation.
 
8. Client Care &amp; Communication
WHAT YOU LEARN: Professional client interaction, comfort management during treatment, post-treatment advice, building trust through consultation and presence.
WHY IT MATTERS: Body work is intimate work. The client's comfort and trust are not optional extras — they are the difference between a one-time client and a loyal regular.
 
9. Project with Case Histories
WHAT YOU LEARN: An independently researched project connected to documented case histories of body therapy clients you have treated during your training.
WHY IT MATTERS: This component demonstrates that you can think clinically about a client over multiple sessions — the discipline that separates a true body therapist from a technique-only worker.
 
In Summary
The Body Therapy diploma is for people who feel called specifically to the body work side of the profession — the calm presence, the hands-on technique, the wellness mindset. CIDESCO + IAO + EBH credentials mean the qualification travels with you, and the focused 565-hour structure (versus the full 1,200-hour Beauty Therapy diploma) means you complete it sooner while still earning a serious, internationally respected qualification.
 
Official CIDESCO reference: https://cidesco.com/qualifications/

Part 6 — CIDESCO Diploma in Beauty &amp; Spa Management
The CIDESCO Diploma in Beauty &amp; Spa Management is the leadership and business pathway in the EBH catalogue — and the one most aligned to entrepreneurs, future spa owners, and existing therapists ready to step into management. Per CIDESCO it is delivered to a 100% online standard internationally and requires no prior beauty technical training. It is not a clinical or DHA-licensing pathway — it is a business and operational mastery qualification.
 
Accreditations
CIDESCO Switzerland · KHDA Dubai · IAO International · EBH Academy
CIDESCO training hours
Minimum 300 hours of training with a CIDESCO School or National Section, covering all subjects in the CIDESCO Beauty &amp; Spa Management Syllabus (per CIDESCO official)
Examination format
CIDESCO examination consists of three components: Multiple Choice Question (MCQ) paper · PowerPoint Presentation · Business Plan
Career destinations
Spa manager, salon manager, wellness centre operations lead, brand or franchise business roles, entrepreneur and owner-operator, consultant for new spa openings
Pre-requisite
No prior beauty or spa technical training is required for this diploma, although an understanding of the beauty and spa industry is an advantage. CIDESCO designed this pathway specifically for people stepping into management.
 
Full Module Breakdown
Each module below shows what is taught and why it matters — drawn from the official CIDESCO syllabus and the EBH delivery framework.
 
1. The Beauty &amp; Spa Industry — Context &amp; Trends
WHAT YOU LEARN: Industry structure, global market dynamics, regional UAE/GCC market specifics, the wellness movement, competitive landscape, and the trends shaping the future of spa and salon business.
WHY IT MATTERS: You cannot manage a business in an industry you don't understand contextually. Real managers think about market trends, not just today's calendar.
 
2. Spa &amp; Salon Operations Management
WHAT YOU LEARN: Day-to-day operations design — workflow, scheduling, treatment-room utilisation, equipment management, supplier relationships, inventory control, and operational quality standards.
WHY IT MATTERS: The biggest difference between a profitable spa and a struggling one is rarely glamorous — it's operational discipline. This module builds the systems-thinking real managers use.
 
3. Financial Management for Spa Business
WHAT YOU LEARN: Pricing strategy, cost structures, margin analysis, cash flow forecasting, budgeting, basic accounting principles, and reading financial statements for a beauty/spa business.
WHY IT MATTERS: Most failed spas didn't fail at therapy — they failed at money. CIDESCO examines this directly because it's where management careers are made or broken.
 
4. Marketing &amp; Brand Management
WHAT YOU LEARN: Brand positioning, marketing strategy, digital marketing (Instagram, WhatsApp business, web), customer acquisition channels, content strategy, partnerships, and reputation management.
WHY IT MATTERS: In a market like the UAE, brand and marketing are the engine of growth. This module teaches how to actually drive bookings, not just talk about marketing.
 
5. Customer Experience Design
WHAT YOU LEARN: Designing the end-to-end client journey — first contact through treatment through follow-up — service quality standards, complaint handling, retention strategy, and building genuine client loyalty.
WHY IT MATTERS: Customer experience is what separates a transactional salon from a beloved brand. Modern spas live or die on retention.
 
6. Human Resources &amp; Team Leadership
WHAT YOU LEARN: Hiring and onboarding therapists, training and development, performance management, scheduling and team dynamics, building a positive culture, and dealing with difficult HR situations professionally.
WHY IT MATTERS: Therapists are your asset. The managers who learn to attract, develop and keep great therapists build the strongest spas. The ones who don't, hire constantly and underperform.
 
7. Retail Strategy
WHAT YOU LEARN: Building a retail product menu, supplier selection, retail merchandising, the consultation-to-retail conversation, retail commission structures and the operational side of retail in a spa.
WHY IT MATTERS: Retail is one of the most under-developed revenue streams in beauty businesses. Done well it adds significant margin without adding labour cost.
 
8. Health, Safety, Hygiene &amp; Legal Compliance
WHAT YOU LEARN: Health and safety standards for spa and salon environments, hygiene protocols, regulatory awareness, licensing requirements, insurance considerations, and the legal foundations of running a service business in the GCC.
WHY IT MATTERS: Compliance failures can shut a business overnight. Managers must own this proactively, not reactively.
 
9. Spa Design &amp; Atmosphere
WHAT YOU LEARN: Principles of spa space design — flow, lighting, ambience, treatment-room layout, retail display, and the small environmental decisions that meaningfully impact client experience.
WHY IT MATTERS: Spa environment is part of the product. Understanding spatial design helps you both open new spaces and improve existing ones.
 
10. CIDESCO Examination Project — Business Plan
WHAT YOU LEARN: Researching, developing and presenting a complete spa or salon business plan — including market analysis, financial projections, operational design, marketing strategy and a PowerPoint presentation of the plan. Required by CIDESCO as part of the formal examination.
WHY IT MATTERS: This is not an academic exercise. Many of our graduates use this business plan as the genuine launch document for their own venture, or as portfolio evidence in management applications. It is the most directly career-actionable assessment in the entire EBH catalogue.
 
In Summary
The Beauty &amp; Spa Management diploma is the qualification for people who don't want to remain on the treatment table forever — who want to lead, to build, to own. CIDESCO-credentialled and accepted globally, with the business plan project that has become the launch document for genuine new businesses. If your ambition is to be the person who hires the therapists rather than just the one giving treatments, this is your pathway.

Category A: General Academy FAQ
Foundational questions about the academy — who we are, where we are, how we teach, what makes us different.
 
Q1.  What is EBH Academy and what makes you different from other beauty schools in Dubai?
EBH Academy — Elite Body Home Academy — is a Dubai-based academy for beauty, wellness and aesthetic professionals. What genuinely sets us apart is not one thing but a combination of structural choices. We keep our diploma batches small (typically up to around ten students) because beauty therapy is a hands-on craft that cannot be learned in a crowded room. Our teaching team is deliberately multi-disciplinary — DHA-approved staff, CIDESCO-certified Beauty Therapy trainers, medical doctors for clinical depth, a physiotherapist for body mechanics, a dietitian for the wellness conversation, and an MBA / marketing expert for business strategy. Our practical training happens in a clinic-style environment with real models, not just demonstrations. And the credentials our graduates leave with — CIDESCO Switzerland, KHDA Dubai, IAO International, EBH Academy — are internationally recognised and built to travel. You will not be a number here. You will be supported, challenged, mentored, and prepared to walk into the UAE beauty industry with the calm confidence of someone who has trained to a serious international standard.
 
Q2.  Where is EBH Academy located and how do I visit?
Our academy is in Dubai and we strongly encourage every prospective student to visit before deciding. The best way to do this is through a Free Counselling Session and Academy Tour — about 30 to 45 minutes, completely free of charge, with no pressure to enrol on the same day. You will see the classrooms, the practical lab, meet members of our teaching team, and have your specific questions answered in person. Most students who visit tell us afterwards that the visit gave them more clarity in one hour than weeks of online research. We would love to welcome you in — shall we set a slot?
 
Q3.  How many students are in each class and why does class size matter?
Our diploma classes are deliberately small — typically up to around ten students per batch. This is a conscious structural decision, not a happy accident. In a hands-on profession like beauty therapy, the gap between someone who has practised a technique eighty times and someone who has practised it twelve times is enormous, and class size is what decides that. Ten students is the size at which the trainer can still see every pair of hands, correct every angle before it becomes a habit, and notice when a student is quietly struggling. It is also the size at which your classmates become real friends and a lifelong professional network. Bigger feels efficient on paper; smaller is what actually produces working professionals. This is one of the reasons our graduates feel ready when they walk into their first interview.
 
Q4.  Who teaches at EBH Academy?
Our teaching team is intentionally multi-disciplinary because the modern beauty industry demands more than one perspective. You will learn from Dubai Health Authority-approved staff who understand the realities of practising under DHA regulation; from CIDESCO-certified Beauty Therapy trainers who themselves hold the Swiss-headquartered qualification you are working toward; from medical doctors who bring clinical depth to anatomy, physiology and contraindications; from a physiotherapist who teaches body mechanics and the technique that protects both your client and your own body across a long career; from a dietitian who brings the nutritional and wellness context that elevates a therapist into a wellness advisor; and from an MBA-qualified marketing expert who teaches the commercial and business side of running a profitable practice. This combination is one of the most quietly powerful things about an EBH education — you will graduate able to speak credibly across every dimension of the modern wellness conversation, not just the technical one.

Sports massage is performance-focused: pre-event protocols prepare specific muscle groups for activity, post-event protocols accelerate recovery and reduce soreness, and between-training-cycle work supports an athlete's ongoing performance. There is significant overlap in technique, which is why many working therapists train in both. From a career angle in the UAE market: wellness spa work tends to favour broad deep-tissue ability, while corporate-fitness, hotel-gym and athlete-clientele settings increasingly request sports massage specifically.

Category G: Certification FAQs
CIDESCO, KHDA, IAO, EBH, DHA — what each means, what each opens, what each does NOT promise. Verification routes. International recognition realities.
 
Q1.  What is CIDESCO and why does it matter for my career?
CIDESCO — Comité International D'Esthétique et de Cosmétologie — is a Swiss-headquartered beauty therapy qualification founded in 1946 with its head office in Zurich. It is widely considered one of the gold-standard credentials in the global beauty industry. Its recognition spans many countries, and in markets like the GCC, parts of Europe and Asia, serious employers and certain professional registration pathways recognise it by name. CIDESCO matters for your career because of portability and credibility: it signals to employers, clients and overseas markets that you have completed rigorous structured training to a verified international standard, rather than holding a generic certificate that may or may not mean much. In a market increasingly crowded with short-course certificates, CIDESCO is the credential that consistently stands out. You can verify any CIDESCO-accredited school through the official register on cidesco.com.
 
Q2.  What is KHDA and what does a KHDA-attested certificate mean?
KHDA — the Knowledge and Human Development Authority — is the regulator of education and training providers in Dubai. A KHDA-attested EBH Academy certificate confirms that the qualification has been issued by an accredited training provider operating within Dubai's regulatory framework, which is the local credential that demonstrates legitimacy within the UAE. KHDA attestation matters because it distinguishes us from unregulated training providers and gives the credential local standing for UAE employment purposes. Not every short course is KHDA-eligible — the verified certification matrix in this document shows specifically which of our courses carry KHDA attestation.
 
Q3.  What is IAO and how is it different from CIDESCO or KHDA?
IAO — the International Accreditation Organization — is a US-based international accreditation body that provides recognition across many fields including beauty, wellness and vocational training. Where KHDA is Dubai-specific and CIDESCO is beauty-therapy-specific, IAO provides a broader international recognition layer that adds credibility, particularly useful for students who may work in markets less familiar with CIDESCO or KHDA. Most of our courses carry IAO accreditation per our verified certification matrix, which gives our students a credential with international portability beyond just the regional or specialised credentials.
 
Q4.  Are all your courses CIDESCO accredited?
Honestly, no — and we want to be clear about that. CIDESCO accreditation applies specifically to our three diplomas: Diploma in Beauty Therapy, Diploma in Body Therapy, and Diploma in Beauty &amp; Spa Management. Our shorter specialised courses (massage modalities, electrical facial, hydrafacial, dermaplaning, lashes, makeup, hair styling) carry KHDA / IAO / EBH Academy credentials per our verified matrix — but not CIDESCO. This is not unusual — CIDESCO certifies comprehensive beauty therapy and spa management programmes that meet their structured curriculum requirements, not every individual technique course. Your counsellor will walk through the specific accreditations attached to whichever course you are considering so you have the accurate picture before deciding.
 
Q5.  Which courses are DHA-eligible and which are not?
Important clarity: of all our courses, only the Diploma in Beauty Therapy is DHA-eligible — meaning a graduate may pursue the DHA licensing examination (DHA itself awards the license based on their own examination, eligibility criteria and decision). The Diploma in Body Therapy and Diploma in Beauty &amp; Spa Management are not DHA clinical-licensing pathways. All our shorter specialised courses (massage, facials, lashes, makeup, hair etc.) are also not DHA pathways. An EBH Academy certificate confirms that a student has successfully completed the course requirements, including the relevant assessment, and meets the standards expected of the qualification. That said, specific employment in any country always depends on that country's own current registration and immigration framework, which evolves over time. The diploma certificates themselves do not expire — your CIDESCO, KHDA, IAO and EBH Academy credentials remain valid as confirmation that you completed the qualification to the relevant standard. Q9.  What is the difference between CIDESCO and CIBTAC?
Both are respected international beauty therapy qualifications. CIDESCO is the Swiss-headquartered qualification founded in 1946 — its head office is in Zurich. Its examination standards are rigorous, its global member-school network spans many countries, and in markets like the GCC, parts of Europe and Asia it is often the qualification employers ask for by name.
